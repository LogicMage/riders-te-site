So how’d you get into ROM hacking originally?

And I’m like - “oh, what’s that game?” And then I see footage of it or whatever bits of footage existed at the time, because it was basically none.
Kid Chameleon
And I was like, “wow, this game is incredible!” And then they’re like, “yeah, you can’t play it. You can’t emulate it.” I’m like, “what the hell? Why can’t you?” And they’re like, “no one knows how to. It’s an arcade game.” I’m like, yeah, I should start looking into it. I met with a few communities, maybe you’re familiar with ZenHacks? That’s a very good community for helping with reverse engineering. The people at the arcade community helped me get an arcade machine set up and ready. Shout out to NaomiMod. They kind of explained a lot of fundamentals of it. You know, just like you got to understand what you’re looking for. A model is made up of vertices and meshes and, you know, music’s made up of this and that. Like, if you know your pieces, even if you’re a novice, you’ll have some idea of what you’re digging through. But the point is that, you know, I learned how to do that for Ollie King.
Kid Chameleon
I wrote scripts for model extraction, animation extraction, texture extraction, like the whole smorgasbord here. And I got into it that way. And then I came into the Riders Discord at some point because I wanted to advertise Ollie King. This guy named Joeatron… he was like, “oh, you know how to program?” And I’m like, “yeah.” He’s like, “want to learn assembly?” I’m like, “okay.” And then I was like, “oh, this isn’t so bad.” Then I just kind of kept going from there.
Kid Chameleon
You know, I looked into Skyward Sword one time and I kind of helped with that, but I didn’t post any of my commits, which I should really do because it’s been like a year. And some other people asked me some individual things. So I’ll write them like some Gecko codes, some PowerPC. But yeah, it’s just kind of like taking interest in those games that no one else takes interest in. Because if no one else is going to do it, then you do it, right? It’s not your responsibility, but if you’re interested in it, right, get after it. Who knows if anyone else is going to do it for you in that time. Life’s a learning opportunity - this is one of those cases where you start learning and you start learning skills that are not only really esoteric, but they can actually apply to other things, right? I mean, understanding how these bits and pieces work makes you really good for applying to jobs because a lot of these companies are like, “oh yeah, you know, we’re going to stick you on this codebase and you have no idea what it is, but you better figure it out.” And if you say, “oh, I know how to kind of read things from the ground up,” then it’s a lot more marketable too.
Kid Chameleon
So, I mean, that was not why I learned it initially, but I learned it because I had interest in games that no one else did. And, uh, I really kind of took that far, especially with Riders, especially with Ollie King. You know, those are kind of the two games that I’m mostly known for. Recently - on Riders - I’ve been super active, wrote a bunch of tools, you know, did a lot of the code for TE, even some of the other mods. I think I have at least one bit of code or more in every mod that exists to date now, including Plus, including Future, including that other thing that’s coming out soon. Uh, I’ve written code for it all at this point.

Logic
So a lot of people know you as, “The Guy Who Designs Crazy Gears.” Would you say it’s your approach to tailor the game to fit more in a sort of maximalist philosophy? And do you think competitive Riders works better that way?
Kid Chameleon
Well, I gotta say this. I will say that’s a dated statement. And the reason why is because, you know, I was given a lot of freedom at the beginning when I was a complete novice at design. Like, I liked games, but designing them was something new. Basically, Protag was like, “I’m giving you the reins to have full creativity because I want crazy new ideas.” I’m like, “yes, sir!”
Logic
laughs
Kid Chameleon
I had no restrictions. And the problem was, is that our testers had no idea how to interpret anything that I made. So there wasn’t really any good testing done on them at the time. This was like 2.1 to 2.2 era. And then afterwards, I was like, Oh my God, I’ve got to tone back and really reconsider. So like 2.3 and onward, you know, I started toning it down a lot. And I think I’m at that point where, you know, not only have I designed everything that I could think of low-key, I have a much more controlled process and tighter understanding on what works and what doesn’t work. Like a lot more now, because we were like, Oh my God, this gear is so fast. Oh my God, this gear has got a crazy mechanic. And yeah, I think I learned a lot more from that. The problem is that people who still say that these days don’t play TE anymore because a lot of those people kind of left so I would just, I would not really believe that. But yeah, you can probably ask a lot of people nowadays and the things are a lot more tame, a lot more controlled, I would say.
Kid Chameleon
When it comes to maximalism… it was never really my goal to kind of push it there. It was always my goal to make something that was more inspired. So for example, like you have Crazy Taxi and Hang On, which Crazy Taxi… Oh, I loathed that gear when it first existed to me because like, I saw the 1.4 Direct and then I heard one line that really ticked me off a lot.
Logic
Huh.
Kid Chameleon
It said, “The Crazy also gains about two rings when walking into walls. This is an homage to Crazy Taxi.” I’m like, “no the hell it’s not!” Because in Crazy Taxi, you start grinding into a wall, they start like getting mad at you and like swearing at you, bro.
Logic
There might’ve been some rogue editorializing going on. I almost wonder if the person who scripted that thought it was a reference to when you drop someone off at their destination and you a fare payment.
Kid Chameleon
No, I don’t think so, because- you know what? That reminds me. So in Vanilla, when you hit somebody, you get rings on Crazy Taxi. I’m like 99% sure that happens because it’s a Vanilla flag. But the point is, is that I’m pretty sure that dropping someone off and getting money, actually the reference for that was that attack that gives you money. So when you hit someone, you technically get into the space where you pick them up and you get the money, you know, or you drop them off and you get the money. I’m pretty sure that’s the callback that they’re trying to do with that one. But I would say like with Crazy Taxi, for example, I was like, wow, this design literally does boost really fast for an expensive amount of rings and bonks into walls and then tricks. So sure, the airtime thing makes sense because of the trick payout, that part was fine. But bonking into walls didn’t. And the boost was too expensive. So the gear was really bad… and me being the creative novice that I was, I was like, why don’t we give it a Crazy Dash or Limiter Cut? Whenever I make a reference to something, I always play it to make sure that I understand what the source material I’m drawing from from is. Watching footage is fine, playing is better…
Logic
As you should.
Kid Chameleon
Yeah, yeah, no, I mean, absolutely. But you know, I was like, why don’t we give it Limiter Cut? And I didn’t really understand it at first, because I was like, oh, you hit brake and then you hit drive. No, you hit reverse and you hit drive. But we don’t really have that in Riders. So I guess I substituted brake back then. And that was cool and all. But then it didn’t really work. Because you know, if you brake normally on a normal gear, it’s too slow. So then I figured out, oh, Crazy Stop exists. So you can stop on a dime and made the Gear work that way, right? And that was more inspired to me. But you know, our testers didn’t really know at the time how to kind of do that. Or like, like, why would I even brake, man? No, it doesn’t make sense to brake to boost. That doesn’t make sense.
Kid Chameleon
Hang-On was even more abstract. That was a gear with a manual transmission. It was kind of nuts. You know, you switch your level, you switch your, your stats. And it’s like, oh, why not stay at the highest level? It’s got too much cost. Why not stay at the lowest level? Well, it doesn’t go that fast, but has low cost. So it became too abstract, right?
Logic
To be fair to the testers, they’re both pretty fundamentally different ways of playing Riders, right?
Kid Chameleon
Yeah. And that was kind of the problem is the thing, like, it’s not their fault that they didn’t understand it. It’s kind of my fault for making Gears that were really crazy. But then again, they did pave the way for more sensible designs that could also be high concept later on. So I think it worked out in the end, because those two designs got toned down and are in such a really stable state right now that I think it all worked out. But at the beginning, it was really experimental. I think, you know, with a lot of work, Riders can be kept at a happy medium. I don’t want minimalism in my riders. You know, there’s, there’s like Plus out there with really simple designs, and they don’t really offer a lot of like, fun gameplay loops, I would argue.
Kid Chameleon
I would say like a happy medium means that you can make gears with rewarding gameplay loops that wants you to explore a little bit more, rather than like the maximalist side where you’re given a million options on a gear or an archetype, and then you’re like, “what do I do with this?” Or minimalism, where you’re given one option, but it has one designated outcome. And it’s like, well, that’s not really fun.
Logic
I guess using the modern Taxis as a sort of litmus test, would you say - this is kind of touching on what you said earlier with the minimalist versus maximalist argument - would you say the game right now is in kind of a nice happy medium as far as those two extremes… kind of in a good spot in the spectrum right now?
Kid Chameleon
You know, I would say yes. Problem is that right now we’re really deep in testing and we have a very fundamentally different game than what’s currently available. So in terms of current live patch, I think mechanically, the game is in like a pretty decent state, right? A lot of stuff’s been toned down, a lot of stuff’s been reworked to be simpler, easier to understand. Rail MI used to exist the way it was, which was the only thing in the game that I could refer to that had like… calculus involved. It was like a hyperbola in there. Because the way that it worked is the slower the rail, the faster the MI bonus was on top. And I’m like, how are we going to explain this to people? Like in a mathematical sense, so they can know how much this works.
Kid Chameleon
So we trashed that. And then we made it up to a certain number per stage, right? Based on the MI percentage, it gives you this much extra, which was a lot simpler, right? There used to be other stuff in the game where it was like, really, really specific, whereas like fly hoops, for example, it took your literal top speed, and then used it for the fly hoop speed calculation, which made certain gears too strong. We made it so that depending on the level, it just uses a certain speed for that calculation instead, right? So I think after a lot of like, simplifications of things, mechanically, where we’d previously gone too far, it’s in a much better spot. And as far as development right now, we’re in a really good cycle, I would say with 2.5. Now it’s a very fundamentally different game. We did a lot of ripping and tearing. But the product of it all… I think it’s probably the best thing we’ve come up with in a long time. We say that about every Riders patch, but now this one…
Logic
laughs
Kid Chameleon
I don’t know how to describe it without spoiling it. But we just did a lot of terraforming. We made a lot of fundamental reworks to certain things… before, there were certain annoying things that like really hurt, like imagine getting hit and losing a race off the second lap. That’s pretty much gone now because of certain things we did to a certain archetype, right? Imagine losing a race to RNG. That’s gone now. Because we got, well, this one thing, I’m gonna say, I don’t know if I can, but we got rid of it. It’s just straight up gone. It was a heated discussion for a while. But it’s crazy how getting rid of that one thing changed the game so much for the better.
